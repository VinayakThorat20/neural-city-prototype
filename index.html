WHY THIS DATASET?

Neural City's street-level scores capture physical infrastructure quality — road condition, walkability, cleanliness. This is powerful but misses two crucial liveability dimensions:

  • Sanitation governance — How well does the ULB manage waste, toilets, and public hygiene? Swachh Survekshan captures this via a 7500-point assessment covering 10+ parameters across 4,300+ cities.
  • Air quality — Outdoor air quality directly impacts health and quality of life. CPCB's city-wise annual AQI data fills this gap.

Together, they create a 360° liveability picture that helps both citizens understand their city holistically and municipal officers identify their weakest dimension for targeted improvement.

50-WORD EXPLANATION
"Swachh Survekshan is India's largest government sanitation audit covering 4,300+ cities annually. Combined with CPCB air quality data, it adds the 'invisible' dimensions of governance and environment that street cameras cannot capture. This enables fair city comparisons beyond physical infrastructure, helping officers identify specific weaknesses and citizens make informed residential and policy decisions."

DATA CLEANING & TRANSFORMATION STEPS
1
SOURCE — Swachh Survekshan 2024–25 (data.opencity.in)
Downloaded the "Million Plus Cities" CSV from data.opencity.in. Raw columns: City, State, SS_Rank, SS_Score (out of 7500), Category. Some cities had missing scores for lower population brackets — we filtered to Million+ cities only for consistency.
2
SOURCE — CPCB Annual AQI (cpcb.nic.in)
Extracted city-wise annual average AQI from CPCB's published PDF bulletins (2024). Converted PDF tables to structured JSON using Python's pdfplumber. AQI values were averaged across all monitoring stations per city to get a city-level representative value.
3
NORMALIZATION — All scores to 0–100 scale
Swachh Score: divided by 75 (max 7500 → 100). AQI Score: inverted using formula (300 − AQI) / 2.5, clamped to [0,100] so lower AQI = higher score. Neural City Street Score: already 0–100 scale from ranking.neuralcity.in.
4
COMPOSITE INDEX — Weighted aggregation
Composite = 0.40 × Swachh_Norm + 0.35 × Street_Score + 0.25 × AQI_Score. Weights reflect that sanitation governance is the primary differentiator, street quality is Neural City's core strength, and AQI provides environmental context. Cities ranked by composite score.
5
DATA MERGING — City name standardization
Swachh dataset uses "Bruhat Bengaluru", CPCB uses "Bangalore", Neural City uses "Bengaluru". Created a city name alias mapping dictionary to join all three datasets on a canonical city ID. Final dataset: 20 cities with complete data across all 3 sources.
6
OUTPUT — JSON + CSV for dashboard consumption
Final structured data saved as cities.json and cities.csv. Each record contains: city_id, city_name, state, ss_score, ss_rank, aqi_annual, aqi_category, street_score, composite_score, composite_rank. This prototype uses the embedded JSON directly in JavaScript.
USE CASE NARRATIVE
FOR MUNICIPAL OFFICERS
A commissioner in Pune sees that the city ranks 6th in street quality but only 14th in Swachh score. This signals: street infrastructure is improving but waste management governance needs attention. They can benchmark against Indore (rank 1 in Swachh) to adopt specific waste management practices. Without this cross-dimensional view, they'd only see the street score and miss the governance gap.
FOR CITIZENS
A family considering relocating between Hyderabad and Ahmedabad can now compare not just road quality but also sanitation standards and air quality in a single dashboard. The composite score gives an at-a-glance answer while individual dimensions let them weigh what matters most — a parent may prioritise AQI for children's health over street walkability.
